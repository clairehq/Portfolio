Context

This project is about researching a domain of group and then designing a complex, interactive user interface that is elegant, detailed, and meets the goals of both client and user. The purpose of this project is about practicing skills such as researching, proposing, doing mockups and userbility test.

Domain of Group

Our domain of group is young people in their 20s.

Research Process

We did some research online focusing on what kinds of activities young people usually do, and what kinds of conflicts they might encounter with. After the research, each of us came up with one idea.

App Concept

We discussed each idea and decided to design an interactive system that helps young people to decide what movie to watch at home based on their preferences. We realized it’s hard and time consuming for many young people to decide what movie to watch on their date or home party. Through this app, they can save time on deciding the movie and find a movie that everyone is interested in.

Persona

We created a persona based on our design focus.

Mockups

We designed an application called Movie Hat. It is a movie picker App. Users create their profiles with different preferences. Depends on whom they watch with and their mood, they choose certain movie preferences. Then, users interact with the app by shaking the smartphones to start the movie suggestion process. The app makes it easier for couples or group of friends to decide what they gonna watch together.

Userbility Test

We found 3 evaluators around 20s that use ios and Android mobile devices to do the user testing. The testing method we used is Heuristic Evaluation. We decided a set of tasks that evaluators need to perform while navigating the app:

  1. To login or sign up to a personal account
  2. Create a new sorting room and choose certain preferences
  3. Join an existing room with their preferences
  4. Shake and come up a final movie
  5. Log out

We observed and took note of the problems that evaluators encountered when performing above tasks. Then we used Jakob Nielsen's usability principles to rate the problems on a scale from 0 to 4 based on their severity.

Results and Interpretation

Throughout the evaluation, we found some usability problems that breaks heuristic rules.

Severity Rating 4:

⚫ Major problems with Flexibility and Efficiency of use issue for search room interface: When invited partner/friends to join an existing room, there is no button for partner/friends to choose their movie preferences before joining an existing room.

⚫ Major problem with Problem with Consistency and Standards for profile interface: In the profile interface, there is no logout button for users to check out their account.

Severity Rating 3:

⚫ Significant problem with System Visibility Status and Consistency and Standards for editing user’s preferences: After users enter a sorting room, there is no button for them to check their preference or edit it, unless go back to the profile page. The shared room also does not display what filters were applied.

Severity Rating 1:

⚫ Minor problem with Visibility of System Status during movie selection: After users select the movies they wanted to watch among those five movies that the app generates for them and tap the confirm button, there is no indicators that tell users to wait for other room members finish selecting.
